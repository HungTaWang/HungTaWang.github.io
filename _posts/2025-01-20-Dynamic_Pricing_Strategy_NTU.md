> Awarded with the Distinguished Paper Award at the 2025 International Conference on Management Innovation and Operations Research (IC-MIOR).

![Post]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/res/post.png){:height="50%" width="50%"}

Perishable products, such as airline tickets or hotel rooms, require precise demand forecasting and effective pricing within a limited sales period. In this research, I proposed a dynamic pricing model that accounts for customer heterogeneity and reference price effects.

Key highlights of the project:

- **Simulated Customer Behavior**: Modeled how different market segments respond to price changes over time.
- **Reference Price Effect**: Integrated psychological factors where customers' past price perceptions influence their current buying decisions.
- **Optimization**: Used Markov Decision Processes (MDP) and Deep Learning to find the optimal pricing path to maximize revenue.

This work bridges the gap between theoretical stochastic modeling and practical management applications, providing a robust framework for revenue management in competitive industries.
