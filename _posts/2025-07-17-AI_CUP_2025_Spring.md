我們 TEAM 7204 最終於 Private Leaderboard 取得 0.835080 的成績，位列第三名。傳統的單一預測模型往往難以同時兼顧多維度且異質的分類任務，本方法指出此過度簡化的框架會導致泛化能力不足，進而使測試集預測偏離預期

以下為本方法核心貢獻：

- 方法價值： 專案需在極度不平衡的六軸感測器（加速度計與陀螺儀）時序資料中，精準預測四個異質性目標：性別、持拍手、球齡（play years）與技術分級（level） 。

- 方法實作： 針對目標變數間的關聯性差異，捨棄單一演算法假設，導入混合模型，將結構簡單的二元分類交由樹狀模型處理，並將具備顯著負相關的複雜多元分類轉化為影像辨識任務，以最大化整體的預測效能與模型穩定性，我們建構了一套結合機器學習與深度學習的混合型求解框架，將複雜的時序訊號變數轉化為可運算的數學特徵:
  1. LightGBM (LGBM) 二元預測模組： 專注處理性別與持拍手的二元分類任務 。模型引入統計特徵與頻域特徵，並擴充三軸向量大小，以增強模型對數值波型分佈的感知能力

  2. 時頻轉換與特徵重構： 揚棄純數值輸入的限制，透過傅立葉轉換將六軸一維時間序列訊號轉化為二維光譜圖 。每筆揮拍紀錄最終產出 7 張 RGB 影像（包含六軸獨立訊號與六軸絕對值總和），為深度學習模組提供豐富的時頻特徵輸入

  3. CNN 多元分類： 針對球齡與技術分級的多元分類任務，採用預訓練之 ResNet-18 作為特徵擷取器 。由於兩目標變數呈現顯著負相關，模型採用共享 Backbone 同時服務兩項任務 。此設計不僅能利用負相關性提取互補資訊，更透過共同條件限制，大幅降低單一目標過擬合的可能

  4. 注意力機制融合： 分別對 7 張頻譜圖執行卷積運算後，導入注意力機制計算動態權重，進而進行加權平均，將多維度影像收斂為單一 512 維度之融合特徵 。特徵隨後經由兩個獨立的 Node 輸出最終的選擇機率分佈

**應用價值與結論**

本方法的混合預測架構，成功將時序感測器訊號轉換為統計參數與時頻影像雙軌特徵。透過共享 Backbone 反向傳遞、Dropout 正規化以及多重隨機種子交叉驗證等機制，模型有效克服了訓練集與測試集之間的資料分佈不平衡。這不僅為智慧運動感測資料提供了具備高度數學可驗證性的理論支撐，更在複雜且不平衡的競賽數據環境中，提供泛化能力極佳的分類方法。

Traditional single predictive models often struggle to simultaneously handle multi-dimensional and heterogeneous classification tasks. This methodology indicates that such an oversimplified framework leads to insufficient generalization capability, thereby causing predictions on the test set to deviate from expectations.

The core contributions of this methodology are as follows:

- Methodological Value: The project necessitates the precise prediction of four heterogeneous targets—gender, playing hand, play years, and skill level—from highly imbalanced time-series data collected via six-axis sensors (accelerometer and gyroscope).
- Implementation: Addressing the varying correlations among target variables, this study discards the single-algorithm assumption and introduces a hybrid model strategy. Structurally simple binary classifications are assigned to tree-based models, while complex multi-class classifications with significant negative correlations are transformed into image recognition tasks. To maximize overall predictive performance and model stability, we constructed a hybrid solution framework combining machine learning and deep learning, translating complex time-series signal variables into computable mathematical features:
  1. LightGBM (LGBM) Binary Prediction Module: Focuses exclusively on the binary classification tasks of gender and playing hand. The model incorporates statistical and frequency-domain features, and expands the three-axis vector magnitude (VM) to enhance the model's perceptual capacity regarding numerical waveform distributions.
  2. Time-Frequency Transformation and Feature Reconstruction: Abandoning the constraints of purely numerical inputs, the six-axis one-dimensional time-series signals are transformed into two-dimensional spectrograms via Fourier transform. Each swing record ultimately yields 7 RGB images (comprising the six independent axis signals and the sum of their absolute values), providing the deep learning module with rich time-frequency feature inputs.
  3. CNN Multi-class Classification: For the multi-class classification tasks of play years and skill level, a pre-trained ResNet-18 is utilized as the feature extractor. Given the significant negative correlation between the two target variables, the model employs a shared backbone architecture to serve both tasks simultaneously. This design not only leverages the negative correlation to extract complementary information but also substantially reduces the probability of single-target overfitting through joint conditional constraints.
  4. Attention Mechanism Fusion: After performing convolution operations on the 7 spectrograms individually, an attention mechanism is introduced to calculate dynamic weights for weighted averaging, converging the multi-dimensional images into a single 512-dimensional fused feature. This feature is subsequently routed through two independent nodes to output the final choice probability distributions.

**Actionable Value & Conclusion**

The hybrid predictive architecture of this methodology successfully translates time-series sensor signals into a dual-track feature system comprising statistical parameters and time-frequency images. Through mechanisms such as shared backbone backpropagation, Dropout regularization, and multi-random-seed cross-validation, the model effectively overcomes the data distribution imbalance between the training and test sets. This not only provides highly verifiable mathematical and theoretical underpinning for smart sports sensor data but also delivers a classification approach with exceptional generalization capability within a complex and imbalanced competition data environment.
